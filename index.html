5 Reasons Why You Should Learn How To Play The Guitar

1. Improve Memory and Increase Cognitive Abilities

Most of us have heard that musicians are smart people but if you are like me i just thought they were always smart (maybe...not sure). Then i found out that playing a musical instrument (not just the guitar) improves your grey matter in the brain because you are constantly learning each day,new notes,tabs,music piece and that is good for your brain and memory.

2. Stress Reliver

We have all been there, you had a tough day. Maybe it was at work or maybe you had an argument with your partner. Stress isn't good for you as an individual cause it ages you quickly and can lead to even heart attacks. A lot of people listen to relatable music in order to cope with stress and just escape and there have been scientific studies to support listening to music decreases stress but you know whats better than listening to music ? Playing that said music. You personally get to express yourself through your own music of how you feel and it can be very therapeutic.

3. Improve Motor Skills

Just like video games playing the guitar is very good for your motor skills and hand-to-eye coordination (or is it eye-to-hand ?) but unlike video games the guitar has no negative effects for playing for long periods of time cause you can play sitting down or standing up and the skills you acquire playing the guitar are transferrable to other activities you may do in your life.

4. Gain Confidence

If you are socially awkward then learning how to play the guitar is gonna help you become more confident and increase your social skills. How? you might add, Well once you start learning the guitar you are going to learn songs then some of your friends and family are going to ask you to play for them and you will be embarrassed but you will do it. They will give you encouragement and feedback which will boost your confidence then you will learn more songs and play for more poeple then one day you will realize that you are not afraid to be in front and around people anymore.

5. Women Love It

I mean need i say more on this one ? Women like guitar players (musicians in general). Why you may ask ? Well that's because women just like men who have interesting hobbies and activities its not really exclusive to the guitar it could be sports, music, art, photography, animations, video editing, writing as long as you are not the typical i work, i watch sports and i drink beer kinda of guy then you are golden. If you are woman reading this then God bless you :)

Those are just some of the many benefits you get from playing the guitar so i hope you are motivated now and you are not going to back out from investing into learning to play the acoustic guitar. Sigma DM-ST

The Sigma DM-ST NT is a valuable dreadnought guitar. Without any elaborate features like body bindings, the acoustic guitar provides a solid spruce top for a well-balanced tone.

  • Body Shape: Dreadnought
  • Top: Solid Spruce
  • Back & Sides: Mahogany
  • Neck: Mahogany
  • Price: R4280

Alvarez AD30

The AD30 is an entry level instrument in Alvarez’s mightily praised Artist Series. While it may be the “cheapest” of them all, it still competes quite well with its bigger brothers and sisters on a sound quality level. Produces a massive sound with great craftsmanship

  • Body Shape: Dreadnought
  • Top: Solid Sitka Spruce
  • Back & Sides: Mahogany
  • Neck: Mahogany
  • Price: R4995

Baby Taylor

The guitar that set the standard for the travel guitar market, the enduringly popular Baby Taylor makes a great musical companion. Portable, playable and affordable, the Baby features a solid spruce top and yields impressive volume and tone for its compact, three-quarter-size scale.

  • Body Shape: 3/4 Dreadnought
  • Top: Solid Spruce
  • Back & Sides: Sapele
  • Neck: Sapele
  • Price: R6400

Yamaha FG820

In addition to warmer and stronger sound thanks to the mahogany back and sides, the body binding and fingerboard binding are cream plastic, for an upgraded look. A wealth of color options are available.

  • Body Shape: Dreadnought
  • Top: Solid Spruce
  • Back & Sides: Mahogany
  • Neck: Mahogany
  • Price: R7350

Taylor GS Mini

A marvel of scaled-down design, the GS Mini is a fun little acoustic cannon that has taken the world by storm. Sporting a rich, full voice that belies its compact size, the Mini is ultra-portable, yet just as comfortable to cradle in the comfort of your home, making it the ultimate modern-day parlor guitar.

  • Body Shape: Grand Symphony mini
  • Top: Solid Mahogany
  • Back & Sides: Sapele
  • Neck: Sapele
  • Price: R9650

Martin DRS1

Long a pioneer in using sustainable materials in their guitars, Martin continues this tradition with the DRS1. With its distinctive solid sapele (a close relative of mahogany) top, back, and sides, the DRS1 gives you bright, clear trebles, and warm midrange.

  • Body Shape: Dreadnought
  • Top: Solid Sapele
  • Back & Sides: Solid Sapele
  • Neck: Sapele
  • Price: R14590
